To find $v_1$ and $v_2$, we apply Ohm's law and Kirchhoff's voltage law. Assume that current $i$ flows through the loop. From Ohm's law,

$$
v_1=2 i, \quad v_2=-3 i
$$


Applying KVL around the loop gives

$$
-20+v_1-v_2=0
$$


We obtain:

$$
-20+2 i+3 i=0 \quad \text { or } \quad 5 i=20 \quad \Rightarrow \quad i=4 \mathrm{~A}
$$


Substituting $i$ finally gives

$$
v_1=8 \mathrm{~V}, \quad v_2=-12 \mathrm{~V}
$$
