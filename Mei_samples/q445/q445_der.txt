Using the current divider rule:

$$
I_1=\frac{R_2 I}{R_2+R_1}=\frac{(1 \Omega)(6 \mathrm{~A})}{1 \Omega+2 \Omega}=2 \mathrm{~A}
$$


The voltage $V_1$ is

$$
V_1=I_1 R_1=(2 \mathrm{~A})(2 \Omega)=4 \mathrm{~V}
$$

and, applying Kirchhoff's voltage law,

$$
+V_s-V_1-20 \mathrm{~V}=0
$$

and

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_s & =V_1+20 \mathrm{~V}=4 \mathrm{~V}+20 \mathrm{~V} \\
& =24 \mathrm{~V}
\end{aligned}
$$
