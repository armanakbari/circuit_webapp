Solution: There are two options for solving this problem. The first is to use Eq. (6.9) as follows:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{1}{R_T} & =\frac{1}{6 \Omega}+\frac{1}{24 \Omega}+\frac{1}{48 \Omega}=0.1667 \mathrm{~S}+0.0417 \mathrm{~S}+0.0208 \mathrm{~S} \\
& =0.2292 \mathrm{~S}
\end{aligned}
$$
and

$$
R_T=\frac{1}{0.2292 \mathrm{~S}}=4.363 \Omega
$$

with

$$
I_1=\frac{R_T}{R_1} I=\frac{4.363 \Omega}{6 \Omega}(42 \mathrm{~mA})=30.54 \mathrm{~mA}
$$
